# Purpose

Complexity Theory studies the computational resources required to solve problems, including time, memory, communication, energy and scalability.

Within the QAI Computing Foundations, complexity theory guides the design of scalable data structures, optimization algorithms and distributed computing architectures capable of handling extremely large world models, digital twins and autonomous multi-agent systems.

Rather than only reducing execution time, QAI seeks to optimize the complete computational lifecycle including memory movement, communication overhead and distributed synchronization.
