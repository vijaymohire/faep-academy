# Overview

This template provides a standardized structure for developing practical laboratories across the FAEP (Federated Autonomous Ecosystem Platform) ecosystem.

Laboratories provide hands-on engineering experiences that enable learners to apply theoretical knowledge, develop practical skills, validate engineering concepts, and gain confidence in designing, building, testing, and operating enterprise-scale Quantum Artificial Intelligence (QAI) solutions.

Laboratories are designed to bridge the gap between theory and real-world engineering practice.

# AI Authoring Guidelines

When creating laboratories:

- Focus on practical engineering.
- Encourage experimentation.
- Use realistic enterprise scenarios.
- Explain concepts before implementation.
- Promote systems thinking.
- Align with learning objectives.
- Avoid proprietary algorithms or confidential information.
- Reference public FAEP repositories where appropriate.
- Include validation activities and expected outcomes.

# FAEP Perspective

Within FAEP, laboratories are practical engineering environments where learners transform theoretical knowledge into professional capability.

Each laboratory integrates concepts from systems engineering, enterprise architecture, Artificial Intelligence, Quantum Artificial Intelligence, Digital Twins, cloud computing, and related technologies while encouraging experimentation, validation, collaboration, and continuous improvement.

Laboratories emphasize engineering excellence, multidisciplinary thinking, and enterprise readiness rather than isolated technical exercises.
