# Overview

This template provides a standardized structure for documenting technical references across the FAEP (Federated Autonomous Ecosystem Platform) ecosystem.

Reference documents provide authoritative descriptions of concepts, technologies, standards, architectures, frameworks, algorithms, data structures, engineering practices, and enterprise knowledge.

Unlike tutorials or laboratories, reference documents are designed for quick lookup, long-term reuse, and consistent technical guidance.

This template is particularly important because it will underpin the future QAI Body of Knowledge (QAI-BoK).

One distinction I'd make is between a Reference and a Tutorial:

Tutorial → teaches how to perform a task.
Reference → explains what something is, its properties, relationships, standards, and where it fits.

In other words:

Reference

↓

Definition

↓

Properties

↓

Relationships

↓

Standards

↓

Examples

↓

Related Resources

A reference should be something that engineers return to repeatedly while working.

# AI Authoring Guidelines

When creating references:

- Be objective and factual.
- Explain concepts before details.
- Maintain consistent terminology.
- Avoid vendor bias where practical.
- Avoid proprietary algorithms or confidential information.
- Cross-reference related FAEP repositories.
- Keep information modular and reusable.

# FAEP Perspective

Within FAEP, reference documents serve as authoritative engineering knowledge assets that provide common terminology, consistent architectural understanding, and reusable technical guidance across the ecosystem.

They establish a shared vocabulary for enterprise architecture, systems engineering, Artificial Intelligence, Quantum Artificial Intelligence, Digital Twins, cloud computing, and multidisciplinary engineering while supporting education, research, enterprise modernization, and future publications such as the QAI Body of Knowledge (QAI-BoK).

Reference documents are intended to remain stable over time while evolving alongside advances in technology and engineering practice.
