# Purpose

The Knowledge Architecture defines how educational knowledge is represented, organized, connected, governed, and shared throughout the FAEP Academy.

It establishes a coherent knowledge ecosystem that transforms educational information into reusable, discoverable, and meaningful knowledge assets supporting education, research, innovation, competency development, and lifelong learning.

The Knowledge Architecture is implementation independent and technology neutral.

This is probably the most important architecture document after the Academy Reference Architecture because knowledge is really the core asset of the FAEP Academy.

I would distinguish it clearly from the Information Architecture:

Information Architecture → organizes educational information.
Knowledge Architecture → transforms information into connected, reusable, discoverable educational knowledge.

Think of it as:

Information
     │
     ▼
Knowledge
     │
     ▼
Understanding
     │
     ▼
Learning
     │
     ▼
Competency

This keeps both documents complementary instead of overlapping.

# Knowledge Domains

The Academy manages multiple categories of knowledge.

Represents courses, learning materials, laboratories, tutorials, educational pathways, and instructional resources.

## Competency Knowledge

Represents competency frameworks, capability models, professional skills, learning outcomes, and workforce readiness.

---

## Research Knowledge

Represents research methodologies, publications, innovation, experiments, collaborative projects, and scientific knowledge.

---

## Institutional Knowledge

Represents educational policies, governance, standards, quality assurance, and organizational knowledge.

---

## Professional Knowledge

Represents industry practices, certifications, professional development, and lifelong learning resources.
