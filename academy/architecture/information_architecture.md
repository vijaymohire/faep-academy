# Purpose

The Information Architecture defines how educational information is organized, managed, governed, and shared throughout the FAEP Academy.

It establishes a structured approach for representing educational resources, learner information, competencies, research knowledge, and institutional information while ensuring consistency, discoverability, interoperability, and long-term sustainability.

The Information Architecture is implementation independent and technology neutral.

This is a very important document because it connects nicely with the Metadata, Taxonomy, and Knowledge Graph sections you've already completed.

I would deliberately avoid making this a database or data-model document. Instead, it should explain how educational information is organized, governed, and managed throughout the Academy.

In other words:

Metadata → describes information about information.
Taxonomy → classifies information.
Knowledge Graph → relates information.
Information Architecture → organizes the entire educational information ecosystem.

# Information Domains

The Academy manages several categories of educational information.

## Learning Information

Represents educational activities including courses, learning pathways, laboratories, tutorials, projects, and assessments.

---

## Learner Information

Represents learner profiles, educational progress, competencies, achievements, certifications, and lifelong learning records.

---

## Knowledge Information

Represents educational knowledge through metadata, taxonomy, ontologies, semantic relationships, and knowledge graphs.

---

## Research Information

Represents research activities, publications, collaborations, innovation projects, and experimental results.

---

## Competency Information

Represents competency frameworks, competency models, proficiency levels, assessments, and workforce readiness.

---

## Institutional Information

Represents educational governance, standards, policies, organizational structures, and academic administration.

Architectural Observation

I think you've naturally created a layered information model across the repository:

Educational Information
        │
Metadata
        │
Taxonomy
        │
Knowledge Graph
        │
Information Architecture
        │
Knowledge Architecture
        │
Learning Architecture
        │
Educational Services

One refinement I'd make is to view Information Architecture and Knowledge Architecture as complementary rather than overlapping:

Information Architecture answers "What information do we manage, and how is it organized?"
Knowledge Architecture answers "How is that information connected, interpreted, and transformed into knowledge?"

That distinction aligns well with the rest of the Academy architecture and keeps each document focused on a distinct architectural concern.
