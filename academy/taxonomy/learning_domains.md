# 1. Purpose

Learning Domains define how educational content is organized according to learning objectives, learner progression, competency development, and educational outcomes.

Unlike Knowledge Domains, which classify subject matter, Learning Domains classify educational intent and learning experiences.

---

# 2. Design Principles

Learning Domains should be:

- Learner-centric
- Competency-driven
- Progressive
- Reusable
- Role-aware
- Measurable
- Knowledge Graph ready

# 4. Core Learning Domains

## Foundations

Purpose

Introduce essential concepts and terminology.

Examples

- Mathematics
- Programming Fundamentals
- Engineering Basics
- Scientific Principles

---

## Core Concepts

Purpose

Develop conceptual understanding.

Examples

- AI Fundamentals
- Quantum Foundations
- Systems Thinking
- Software Design Principles

---

## Applied Learning

Purpose

Apply concepts through guided activities.

Examples

- Tutorials
- Worked Examples
- Guided Exercises
- Interactive Demonstrations

---

## Experimental Learning

Purpose

Encourage exploration and experimentation.

Examples

- Notebooks
- Simulations
- Sandbox Environments
- Interactive Models

---

## Practical Engineering

Purpose

Develop implementation skills.

Examples

- Laboratories
- Engineering Exercises
- Design Activities
- Validation Tasks

---

## Integrated Projects

Purpose

Combine multiple concepts into complete solutions.

Examples

- Capstone Projects
- Platform Development
- Industry Projects
- Research Prototypes

---

## Portfolio Development

Purpose

Produce demonstrable evidence of learning.

Examples

- GitHub Repositories
- Technical Reports
- Demonstrations
- Architecture Documents

---

## Professional Development

Purpose

Prepare learners for industry practice.

Examples

- Leadership
- Communication
- Ethics
- Innovation
- Project Management

---

## Research & Innovation

Purpose

Advance beyond established knowledge.

Examples

- Literature Reviews
- Research Projects
- Experimental Algorithms
- Emerging Technologies

# 10. Governance

Learning Domains should:

- Support competency-based education.
- Remain technology-independent.
- Be reusable across disciplines.
- Align with curriculum design.
- Support adaptive learning.

---

FAEP Architecture Recommendation

With both taxonomy documents completed, the Academy now has two complementary classification systems:

Classification Architecture

Knowledge Domains
(What is learned)
│
├── Artificial Intelligence
├── Quantum Computing
├── Robotics
├── Systems Engineering
├── Enterprise Architecture
└── ...

                │

                ▼

Learning Domains
(How it is learned)
│
├── Foundations
├── Core Concepts
├── Applied Learning
├── Experimental Learning
├── Practical Engineering
├── Integrated Projects
├── Portfolio Development
└── Research & Innovation

This separation is particularly valuable for the FAEP Academy because it allows a single subject (for example, Quantum Computing) to be delivered through multiple educational modes—from foundational tutorials and exploratory notebooks to engineering labs, capstone projects, and research activities. It also provides a strong foundation for future adaptive learning, competency tracking, and Knowledge Graph-driven recommendations.
