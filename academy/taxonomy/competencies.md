# 1. Purpose

This document defines the competency framework used throughout the FAEP Academy.

Competencies represent measurable professional capabilities developed through the integration of knowledge, skills, practical experience, and assessment.

They provide the foundation for curriculum design, learner progression, certifications, career pathways, and AI-driven personalized learning.

---

# 2. Design Principles

Competencies should be:

- Observable
- Measurable
- Outcome-based
- Role-oriented
- Reusable
- Industry-aligned
- Extensible
- Knowledge Graph ready

Architectural Recommendation

With this document completed, the Academy now has a robust educational progression model:

Knowledge Domains
        │
        ▼
Learning Domains
        │
        ▼
Learning Assets
        │
        ▼
Skills
        │
        ▼
Competencies
        │
        ▼
Assessments
        │
        ▼
Certifications
        │
        ▼
Career Paths

One enhancement I'd recommend for the FAEP Academy is to make competencies the central hub of the ecosystem. Rather than simply tagging assets with competencies, model competencies as first-class entities connected to roles, skills, assessments, learning assets, certifications, career paths, and the Knowledge Graph. This creates a many-to-many relationship network that supports adaptive learning, competency gap analysis, personalized recommendations, and workforce development. As the Academy evolves, competencies can become the primary unit for learner profiles, digital credentials, and AI-driven curriculum planning, providing a scalable foundation for both education and professional development.
