# Tacit Knowledge

Develop the knowledge and engineering skills required to capture, preserve, share, and apply tacit knowledge within intelligent engineering systems, organizations, and collaborative ecosystems.

Tacit Knowledge represents the experience, judgment, intuition, contextual understanding, and organizational memory that cannot always be fully documented but plays a critical role in engineering, research, innovation, and decision making.

# Course Overview

Tacit Knowledge Engineering focuses on capturing, organizing, preserving, and applying human expertise that is often gained through experience rather than formal documentation.

Within the FAEP ecosystem, Tacit Knowledge contributes to organizational learning, engineering memory, collaborative intelligence, decision support, knowledge transfer, and continuous improvement.

Learners progress through tutorials, laboratories, notebooks, engineering projects, and the FAEP Client Lab to understand how tacit knowledge can be transformed into reusable engineering assets.

---

# Learning Objectives

After completing this course, learners should be able to:

- Understand the concept of Tacit Knowledge.
- Distinguish between tacit and explicit knowledge.
- Explain organizational memory and engineering experience.
- Apply Tacit Knowledge principles within engineering teams.
- Understand Knowledge Fabrics and Tacit Fabric concepts.
- Develop engineering projects that preserve organizational knowledge.
- Build a professional knowledge engineering portfolio.
