Develop the engineering knowledge and practical skills required to design, integrate, deploy, and operate Hybrid Quantum Systems that combine classical computing, quantum computing, Artificial Intelligence, cloud platforms, and enterprise engineering.

Hybrid Quantum Systems provide the bridge between today's classical computing infrastructure and tomorrow's fault-tolerant quantum computing environments.

# Course Overview

Hybrid Quantum Systems combine classical computing resources with quantum processors, enabling practical solutions that leverage the strengths of both computational paradigms.

This course introduces the engineering principles required to design hybrid architectures, quantum-classical workflows, orchestration platforms, runtime environments, AI integration, cloud-based quantum services, and enterprise deployment strategies.

Learners progress through tutorials, laboratories, notebooks, engineering projects, and the FAEP Client Lab to gain practical hybrid quantum engineering experience.

---

# Learning Objectives

After completing this course, learners should be able to:

- Understand Hybrid Quantum System architectures.
- Explain classical–quantum integration models.
- Understand hybrid quantum workflows.

- Develop hybrid quantum engineering projects.
