# FAEP World Model Engineering

## Overview

The **FAEP World Model Engineering Learning Track** prepares students to design, develop and validate computational world models that represent physical systems, digital environments, enterprises and autonomous ecosystems.

The learning track integrates artificial intelligence, systems engineering, digital twins, simulation, knowledge graphs, robotics and enterprise architecture into a multidisciplinary engineering discipline focused on building models that support perception, reasoning, prediction and planning.

Within the FAEP Academy, World Model Engineering provides the knowledge representation and simulation capabilities that underpin advanced intelligent systems and federated autonomous ecosystems.

I think this is one of the most exciting engineering tracks because it sits at the intersection of AI, digital twins, knowledge graphs, simulation and systems engineering.

One suggestion is to frame World Model Engineering as the engineering discipline for creating computational representations of complex environments that support perception, reasoning, prediction, planning and decision support. This keeps the curriculum grounded in engineering while leaving room for future advances.

---

# Vision

Develop engineering expertise that enables learners to create scalable, explainable and continuously evolving world models capable of supporting scientific research, intelligent automation, enterprise decision support and autonomous operations.

The long-term objective is to establish World Model Engineering as a foundational discipline for future intelligent platforms that combine structured knowledge, simulation and adaptive reasoning.
