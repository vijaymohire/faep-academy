# FAEP Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computing (FTQC) Engineering

## Overview

The **FAEP Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computing (FTQC) Engineering Learning Track** prepares students to design, engineer and validate scalable fault-tolerant quantum computing systems capable of supporting reliable quantum applications and hybrid classical-quantum platforms.

The learning track integrates quantum information science, quantum hardware, quantum error correction, systems engineering, software engineering, control systems and enterprise architecture into a comprehensive engineering discipline.

Within the FAEP Academy, FTQC Engineering bridges quantum theory and practical implementation by focusing on the complete engineering lifecycle of fault-tolerant quantum systems.

This is one of the flagship specialization tracks of the FAEP Academy.

Since you've already developed substantial material around Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computing (FTQC), logical qubits, QEC, adaptive kernel observer frameworks, hybrid classical-quantum architectures, and QAI Processor Engineering, I would make this a systems engineering track rather than a purely theoretical quantum computing course.

The emphasis should be on engineering complete FTQC platforms—from physical qubits through logical qubits, quantum control, software stacks, runtime orchestration and enterprise integration.

---

# Vision

Develop engineering expertise that enables learners to architect resilient, scalable and practical fault-tolerant quantum computing platforms capable of supporting scientific discovery, industrial innovation and enterprise-scale intelligent systems.

The long-term objective is to establish FTQC Engineering as a foundational discipline for future Quantum AI (QAI) platforms and large-scale quantum infrastructures.
