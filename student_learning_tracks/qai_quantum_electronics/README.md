# FAEP QAI Quantum Electronics Engineering

## Overview

The **FAEP QAI Quantum Electronics Engineering Learning Track** prepares students to design, analyze and integrate the electronic, photonic and semiconductor technologies that enable practical quantum computing systems.

The learning track combines quantum information science, electrical engineering, semiconductor technology, photonics, cryogenic engineering, quantum control systems and systems engineering into a multidisciplinary engineering discipline.

Within the FAEP Academy, Quantum Electronics Engineering serves as the hardware engineering foundation supporting fault-tolerant quantum computing, Quantum Artificial Intelligence (QAI) platforms and future hybrid classical–quantum systems.


I think this has the potential to become one of the signature engineering tracks of the FAEP Academy.

Unlike a general quantum computing course, QAI Quantum Electronics Engineering should focus on engineering the physical and electronic foundations that enable practical quantum computing systems. It naturally bridges physics, electrical engineering, semiconductor engineering, photonics, cryogenics, packaging and systems engineering.

# Vision

Develop engineering expertise that enables learners to design scalable, reliable and manufacturable quantum electronic systems capable of supporting future quantum processors and enterprise quantum computing platforms.

The long-term objective is to establish Quantum Electronics Engineering as a core discipline connecting device physics, electronic systems and quantum platform engineering.

# Learning Outcomes

Students completing this learning track should be able to:

* Explain the engineering principles behind quantum electronic systems.
* Compare different qubit technologies and implementation trade-offs.
* Design quantum control and readout architectures.
* Evaluate semiconductor, photonic and cryogenic technologies for quantum platforms.
* Apply systems engineering to quantum hardware development.
* Understand the relationship between hardware, runtime software and enterprise platforms.
* Conduct multidisciplinary quantum engineering research.
* Contribute to the development of scalable quantum computing technologies.
