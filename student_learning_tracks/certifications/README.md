# FAEP Certification Framework

## Overview

The **FAEP Certification Framework** provides a structured pathway for validating engineering knowledge, practical skills and professional competencies developed through the FAEP Academy.

The certification program integrates the Mathematical Foundations, QAI Computing Foundations, FAEP Reference Architecture, Engineering Frameworks, Engineering Patterns, Technology Libraries, Standards Library and Student Learning Tracks into progressive professional certifications.

Within the FAEP Academy, certifications emphasize practical engineering capability, research readiness and innovation rather than examination alone.

I actually think this is one area where the FAEP Academy can distinguish itself.

Rather than simply issuing certificates, the Certification Framework should validate a learner's ability to design, build, integrate and innovate. It should sit on top of everything you've already built—Foundations, Frameworks, Patterns, Technologies and Standards.

This makes certifications evidence-based rather than course-completion based.

---

# Vision

Develop an internationally recognized certification framework that validates engineering excellence across artificial intelligence, systems engineering, robotics, quantum computing, enterprise architecture and intelligent platform development.

The long-term objective is to establish FAEP certifications as evidence-based credentials supporting academic achievement, industrial practice and entrepreneurial innovation.

The FAEP Certification Framework establishes a progressive professional credentialing system that recognizes engineering capability from foundational knowledge through advanced research and innovation.

# Contributing

When developing new certification programs:

* Define measurable competency outcomes.
* Align assessments with learning objectives.
* Include practical engineering activities.
* Reference relevant technologies and standards.
* Encourage research and innovation.
* Support continuous professional development.
* Maintain transparent assessment criteria.
* Continuously evolve certifications to reflect advances in engineering practice.
