# FAEP QAGI Engineering

## Overview

The **FAEP Quantum Artificial General Intelligence (QAGI) Engineering Learning Track** prepares students to investigate, design and evaluate architectures that combine artificial intelligence, quantum computing, systems engineering and enterprise-scale intelligent platforms.

The learning track explores engineering methodologies for developing increasingly capable intelligent systems through multidisciplinary integration of AI, hybrid classical-quantum computing, knowledge engineering, digital twins and federated architectures.

Within the FAEP Academy, QAGI Engineering serves as an advanced research and engineering discipline that builds upon established AI and quantum computing foundations while encouraging rigorous experimentation, validation and responsible system design.

I think this should become one of the premier research-oriented tracks in the FAEP Academy.

One suggestion: rather than presenting QAGI as an achieved capability, frame the track as engineering methodologies, architectures, validation strategies and research directions for progressively more capable general intelligence systems. That keeps the curriculum grounded while still supporting your long-term vision.

---

# Vision

Develop engineering expertise that enables learners to design scalable, trustworthy and adaptable intelligent systems capable of solving complex multidisciplinary problems through collaboration between artificial intelligence, quantum technologies and human expertise.

The long-term objective is to advance engineering methodologies that contribute to increasingly capable intelligent systems while maintaining strong foundations in systems engineering, governance and scientific validation.
