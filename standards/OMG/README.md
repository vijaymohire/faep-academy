# FAEP OMG Standards & Modeling Frameworks

## Overview

The **FAEP OMG Standards & Modeling Frameworks Library** provides a structured reference to modeling standards, architecture specifications and interoperability frameworks developed by the Object Management Group (OMG).

OMG standards support enterprise architecture, model-driven engineering, software engineering, systems engineering, digital engineering and business process modeling through standardized modeling languages and interoperable specifications.

Within the FAEP Academy, OMG standards complement the QAI Computing Foundations, FAEP Reference Architecture, Engineering Frameworks, Engineering Patterns and Technology Libraries by providing the modeling foundation for designing, analyzing and managing complex intelligent systems.

I actually think OMG (Object Management Group) is one of the most strategically important standards organizations for the FAEP Academy because it directly supports model-driven engineering, which aligns with much of your repository.

Looking across your work, OMG naturally connects to:

FAEP Reference Architecture
Systems Engineering Patterns
Digital Twins
Enterprise Architecture
Mission Engineering
Knowledge Graphs
World Models
Engineering Frameworks
MBSE

Unlike ISO (governance), IEEE (technical engineering), INCOSE (systems engineering) and NIST (security), I would position OMG as the model-driven architecture and interoperability foundation of the FAEP Academy.

# Vision

Develop a comprehensive OMG standards reference that enables students, engineers and researchers to model, architect and integrate intelligent systems using internationally recognized modeling standards.

The long-term objective is to establish OMG specifications as the model-driven engineering foundation for enterprise-scale intelligent platforms and federated ecosystems.

# Long-Term Vision

The FAEP OMG Standards & Modeling Frameworks Library establishes the model-driven engineering reference for the Federated Autonomous Ecosystem Platform.

By documenting architecture frameworks, modeling languages and interoperability specifications, the library provides practical guidance for designing, analyzing and evolving complex intelligent systems.

The OMG Standards Library emphasizes technology independence by aligning FAEP engineering methodologies with internationally recognized modeling standards, enabling organizations to develop interoperable, architecture-centric and model-driven intelligent platforms.

# Contributing

When adding a new OMG specification:

* Describe the purpose and scope of the specification.
* Explain its relevance to the FAEP architecture.
* Reference related engineering frameworks, patterns and technologies.
* Identify modeling and interoperability considerations.
* Describe relationships with INCOSE, ISO, IEEE and enterprise architecture practices.
* Promote reusable models and platform-independent engineering.
* Reference official OMG specifications.
* Keep content current with new specification releases and revisions.
