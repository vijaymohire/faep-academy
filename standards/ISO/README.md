# FAEP ISO Standards

## Overview

The **FAEP ISO Standards Library** provides a structured reference to international standards published by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) that support the engineering, governance, quality and operational excellence of intelligent systems within the Federated Autonomous Ecosystem Platform (FAEP).

ISO standards provide globally recognized guidance for quality management, systems engineering, software engineering, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, innovation, information management and enterprise governance.

Within the FAEP Academy, ISO standards establish the quality, governance and compliance foundation that complements the QAI Computing Foundations, FAEP Reference Architecture, Engineering Frameworks, Engineering Patterns and Technology Libraries.

I think ISO is the broadest standards body in your repository because it touches almost every engineering discipline you've documented.

# Long-Term Vision

The FAEP ISO Standards Library establishes the enterprise quality and governance reference for the Federated Autonomous Ecosystem Platform.

By documenting internationally recognized ISO standards, the library provides practical guidance for designing secure, interoperable, resilient and enterprise-ready intelligent systems.

The ISO Standards Library emphasizes technology independence by aligning FAEP engineering methodologies with globally recognized quality, governance and lifecycle standards, enabling organizations to deliver intelligent platforms with consistent engineering practices, operational excellence and long-term sustainability.

# Contributing

When adding a new ISO standard:

* Describe the purpose and scope of the standard.
* Explain its relevance to the FAEP architecture.
* Reference related engineering frameworks, patterns and technologies.
* Identify governance, quality and compliance considerations.
* Describe relationships with IEEE, INCOSE, NIST and other standards where applicable.
* Promote technology neutrality and continuous improvement.
* Reference official ISO publications where appropriate.
* Keep content current with revisions and new standards.
