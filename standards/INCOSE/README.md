# FAEP INCOSE Standards

## Overview

The **FAEP INCOSE Standards Library** provides a structured reference to systems engineering principles, lifecycle processes, recommended practices and guidance promoted by the International Council on Systems Engineering (INCOSE).

INCOSE provides internationally recognized systems engineering methodologies that support the planning, design, integration, verification, validation, operation and continuous evolution of complex systems.

Within the FAEP Academy, INCOSE principles complement the QAI Computing Foundations, FAEP Reference Architecture, Engineering Frameworks, Engineering Patterns and Technology Libraries by providing an end-to-end systems engineering perspective.

I actually think INCOSE is probably the most important standards organization for the FAEP Academy.

# Long-Term Vision

The FAEP INCOSE Standards Library establishes the systems engineering reference for the Federated Autonomous Ecosystem Platform.

By documenting systems engineering principles, lifecycle processes and enterprise engineering methodologies, the library provides practical guidance for developing scalable, interoperable and resilient intelligent systems.

The INCOSE Standards Library emphasizes technology independence by aligning FAEP engineering methodologies with internationally recognized systems engineering practices, enabling organizations to manage complexity while maintaining architectural integrity, lifecycle traceability and long-term sustainability.

When adding new INCOSE guidance:

* Explain its relevance to the FAEP architecture.
* Reference related engineering frameworks, patterns and technologies.
* Identify lifecycle activities and traceability considerations.
* Describe relationships with ISO, IEEE, OMG and MBSE practices.
* Promote systems thinking and architecture-centric engineering.
* Reference official INCOSE publications where appropriate.
* Keep content current with evolving systems engineering practices.
