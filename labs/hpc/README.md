# Overview

The **High Performance Computing (HPC) Labs** provide practical learning experiences for designing, developing, deploying, and optimizing large-scale computing systems using the FAEP (Federated Autonomous Ecosystem Platform) ecosystem.

These laboratories introduce learners to modern HPC architectures that combine CPUs, GPUs, NPUs, TPUs, FPGAs, DPUs, Quantum Processing Units (QPUs), cloud computing, edge computing, Digital Twins, and Artificial Intelligence (AI) into intelligent computing fabrics capable of supporting enterprise workloads, scientific computing, engineering simulations, and future Quantum Artificial Intelligence (QAI) platforms.

Rather than treating HPC as isolated supercomputers, FAEP views HPC as an adaptive, intelligent computing ecosystem capable of dynamically allocating resources, optimizing workloads, and supporting autonomous enterprise operations.

I think this is another flagship lab because High Performance Computing (HPC) is the bridge between today's AI infrastructure and tomorrow's Quantum Artificial Intelligence (QAI) infrastructure.

One thing I'd emphasize is that FAEP treats HPC as more than supercomputers. It is a computing fabric that can orchestrate CPUs, GPUs, NPUs, TPUs, DPUs, FPGAs, QPUs, edge devices, cloud clusters, and future quantum processors as a single intelligent computing ecosystem.

That aligns very well with your vision of QAI Cloud, QAI Hub, Hybrid Runtime, and Fabric Computing.

# FAEP Perspective

Within FAEP, High Performance Computing represents the intelligent computing foundation that enables large-scale enterprise applications, scientific research, Digital Twins, Artificial Intelligence, and Quantum Artificial Intelligence to operate as a unified computing ecosystem.

Rather than viewing HPC as isolated hardware, FAEP treats compute resources as members of an adaptive computing fabric where CPUs, GPUs, NPUs, TPUs, FPGAs, DPUs, QPUs, cloud infrastructure, edge devices, and future quantum technologies collaborate through intelligent orchestration, hybrid runtimes, dynamic scheduling, and autonomous resource management.

As the ecosystem evolves from classical computing to hybrid quantum-classical systems and ultimately toward Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computing (FTQC), HPC becomes the scalable computational backbone supporting QAGI and QASI across enterprise, scientific, industrial, and mission-critical environments.
