# Overview

The **Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS)** laboratory provides practical, hands-on learning experiences for designing, developing, validating, and deploying intelligent systems that tightly integrate computation, communication, sensing, control, and physical processes.

Within the FAEP (Federated Autonomous Ecosystem Platform), Cyber-Physical Systems extend beyond traditional embedded control by incorporating Artificial Intelligence (AI), Quantum Artificial Intelligence (QAI), Digital Twins, Enterprise Intelligence, Hybrid Runtime, cloud-edge computing, and autonomous decision-making.

These laboratories demonstrate how intelligent physical systems interact with enterprise platforms while continuously learning, adapting, and improving through real-time feedback.

This is another repository that aligns extremely well with the FAEP ecosystem because Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) naturally bring together almost every technology we've been developing.

Unlike traditional CPS courses that focus on embedded systems and industrial control, I think FAEP should define CPS as intelligent, connected, autonomous systems that integrate physical assets with Digital Twins, Enterprise Intelligence, QAI, Hybrid Runtime, and cloud-edge infrastructures.

One important addition I'd make is that future CPS will not just sense and control—they will reason, collaborate, learn, and adapt using Enterprise Intelligence.

As we've developed the architecture, a pattern has emerged that isn't common in traditional CPS literature: the idea of an Enterprise Cyber-Physical Ecosystem rather than isolated Cyber-Physical Systems.

Instead of each robot, factory, drone, satellite, or autonomous vehicle operating independently, FAEP treats them as members of a federated ecosystem connected through QAI Hub, QAI Cloud, Service Mesh, Enterprise Intelligence, Digital Twins, and Knowledge Graphs. Individual CPS nodes retain local autonomy for real-time operation, while the enterprise layer provides global coordination, shared knowledge, governance, and continuous learning. I think this ecosystem perspective fits naturally with the broader FAEP vision and distinguishes it from conventional CPS architectures that focus primarily on individual systems rather than coordinated intelligent enterprises.
