# Overview

The **QAI Robotics Lab** introduces the engineering principles, architectures, and practical methodologies required to develop next-generation intelligent robotic systems using Artificial Intelligence (AI), Quantum Artificial Intelligence (QAI), Hybrid Computing, Systems Engineering, Digital Twins, Knowledge Graphs, and Enterprise Architecture.

Unlike traditional robotics laboratories that primarily focus on mechanics, electronics, and robot programming, this laboratory adopts a multidisciplinary engineering perspective where robots become intelligent participants within larger enterprise and ecosystem architectures.

The laboratory integrates scientific foundations, engineering practices, hybrid computing, optimization, and intelligent decision making into a unified framework suitable for modern industrial, scientific, and societal applications.

I think the Robotics Lab should become another flagship lab like the QAI Computing Foundations Lab. Rather than being a traditional robotics course, it should present QAI Robotics as an engineering discipline spanning software, cyber-physical systems, enterprise robotics, and federated autonomous ecosystems.

# What is a Robot?

Traditional definitions often describe a robot as a programmable physical machine.

The FAEP perspective is broader.

A robot is:

> **An autonomous or semi-autonomous intelligent system capable of sensing, reasoning, planning, learning, acting, collaborating, and continuously improving within physical, virtual, cyber-physical, or hybrid environments.**

This definition accommodates emerging forms of intelligent automation while remaining applicable to traditional robotics.
