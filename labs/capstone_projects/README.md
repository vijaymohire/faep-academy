# Overview

The **Capstone Projects** repository provides comprehensive, real-world engineering projects that enable learners to integrate knowledge acquired throughout the FAEP (Federated Autonomous Ecosystem Platform) learning journey.

Unlike individual laboratories or tutorials that focus on specific technologies, Capstone Projects combine multiple disciplines including Artificial Intelligence, Quantum Artificial Intelligence (QAI), Enterprise Architecture, Digital Twins, Knowledge Graphs, Enterprise Intelligence, Hybrid Runtime, Runtime Services, Security, Validation, and Systems Engineering into complete end-to-end solutions.

Capstone Projects demonstrate practical engineering competence and prepare learners for enterprise, research, and innovation environments.

This is another repository that I think can become one of the flagship components of the FAEP Academy.

Most educational platforms stop at labs and tutorials. A Capstone Project is different—it demonstrates that a learner can integrate multiple knowledge areas into a complete engineering solution. It should be the culmination of everything learned throughout the academy.

I would eventually introduce three tiers of capstone projects:

Individual Capstones – completed by a single learner to demonstrate mastery of a specific topic.
Team Capstones – multidisciplinary projects where learners assume different enterprise roles (architect, AI engineer, systems engineer, DevSecOps engineer, researcher, etc.), closely mirroring real-world product development.
Industry Capstones – projects based on actual modernization or research scenarios, where learners apply the FAEP frameworks to open-ended problems with measurable outcomes.

That progression would make the academy resemble professional engineering practice, giving students not only technical knowledge but also experience in collaboration, systems thinking, and end-to-end solution delivery. I think it would become one of the strongest educational differentiators of the FAEP ecosystem.
