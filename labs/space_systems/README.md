# QAI Space Systems Engineering Lab

> **Engineering Intelligent Space Systems using Artificial Intelligence, Quantum Computing, Hybrid Computing, Systems Engineering, and the FAEP Ecosystem**

---

# Overview

The **QAI Space Systems Engineering Lab** introduces the scientific, engineering, operational, and enterprise principles required to design, develop, validate, deploy, and operate next-generation intelligent space systems.

Rather than treating space systems as isolated spacecraft or satellites, this laboratory adopts a multidisciplinary engineering perspective where missions become intelligent ecosystems composed of humans, spacecraft, robotics, digital twins, artificial intelligence, quantum services, scientific instruments, enterprise platforms, and autonomous operations.

The laboratory integrates Systems Engineering, Artificial Intelligence, Quantum Artificial Intelligence (QAI), Hybrid Computing, Mission Engineering, Security, Digital Twins, Knowledge Graphs, Robotics, Scientific Computing, and Enterprise Architecture into a unified educational framework.

---

# Vision

To establish a multidisciplinary engineering laboratory that prepares learners, researchers, engineers, and organizations to develop intelligent, resilient, secure, and autonomous space systems capable of supporting scientific discovery, commercial space activities, planetary exploration, and future federated space ecosystems.

---

# Mission

The QAI Space Systems Engineering Lab aims to:

- Develop engineering-oriented space systems knowledge.
- Integrate Artificial Intelligence into mission operations.
- Introduce Quantum-assisted mission engineering.
- Promote hybrid classical and quantum computing.
- Encourage interdisciplinary engineering.
- Support autonomous mission operations.
- Foster scientific research.
- Enable intelligent space infrastructure.
- Prepare learners for future planetary ecosystems.
