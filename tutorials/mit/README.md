# MIT Learning Path

> MIT-inspired learning resources for Engineering, Mathematics, Artificial Intelligence, Quantum Computing, Systems Engineering, and Quantum AI (QAI).

---

# Overview

The MIT Learning Path within the FAEP Academy provides a structured collection of educational resources inspired by concepts taught through the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and related engineering and scientific disciplines.

Rather than duplicating course material, this learning path focuses on understanding fundamental concepts, applying engineering principles, and connecting academic knowledge with practical implementation.

The objective is to help learners develop a strong theoretical foundation while exploring modern applications in Artificial Intelligence, Quantum Computing, Systems Engineering, Robotics, and Quantum AI.

---

# Learning Objectives

After completing this learning path, learners will be able to:

- Develop strong analytical thinking skills
- Understand engineering design principles
- Apply computational thinking to solve complex problems
- Build mathematical foundations for engineering
- Understand optimization techniques
- Explore Machine Learning fundamentals
- Learn Quantum Information Science concepts
- Understand Quantum Computing principles
- Apply Systems Engineering methodologies
- Translate academic concepts into practical engineering solutions

# Getting Started

Whether you are beginning your engineering journey or expanding your knowledge in Artificial Intelligence, Quantum Computing, and Systems Engineering, the MIT Learning Path provides a structured progression from fundamental concepts to advanced interdisciplinary applications.

The recommended approach is to develop strong scientific and engineering foundations before exploring specialized technologies and research topics.

---

## Prerequisites

The following background knowledge is recommended:

- High school mathematics
- Basic programming (Python recommended)
- Logical reasoning
- Curiosity for science and engineering
- Interest in problem solving

No prior experience in Artificial Intelligence or Quantum Computing is required.
