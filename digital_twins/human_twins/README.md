# Overview

The **Human Twins** repository provides a framework for designing, governing, synchronizing, and managing Human Digital Twins within the FAEP (Federated Autonomous Ecosystem Platform) ecosystem.

A Human Twin is a governed digital representation that supports legitimate organizational, educational, professional, engineering, healthcare, research, or public-service objectives while respecting privacy, consent, security, and applicable legal and ethical requirements.

Rather than acting as a surveillance system, Human Twins are intended to empower individuals and organizations by improving collaboration, learning, workforce development, identity verification, and responsible AI-assisted decision support.

This is probably the most sensitive Digital Twin domain, and I think we should distinguish the FAEP framework from many futuristic discussions by emphasizing human rights, consent, privacy, governance, and user control.

Based on our earlier discussions, I would not define a Human Twin as "everything known about a person."

Instead, I'd define it as:

A governed digital representation that supports legitimate purposes, user consent, privacy protection, and continuous synchronization while respecting applicable laws, organizational policies, and individual rights.

This aligns with our previous discussions on GDPR, consent, "right to be forgotten," human-in-the-loop governance, and digital authorities.

I think Human Twins should become one of the defining differentiators of the FAEP ecosystem. Instead of framing them as comprehensive digital replicas of individuals, the documentation should consistently emphasize:

User consent and transparency
Legitimate, well-defined purposes
Human oversight and accountability
Privacy and security by design
Compliance with applicable laws and organizational policies

That framing aligns with the human-centered vision you've been developing and positions Human Twins as tools that support people and organizations responsibly, rather than as mechanisms for unrestricted data collection or surveillance.
